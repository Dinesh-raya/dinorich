Board Layout: How Should the Game Board Feel?

The board is the heart of the game. This is about spatial design and information density.

DINO
RICHUP

A — Classic Grid + Gold Borders

Traditional Monopoly layout — 11x11 grid, tiles on edges, center shows logo + game info. Gold borders on tiles, dark navy fill. Clean, familiar, proven. Center area shows turn status, dice, activity feed.

CENTER
simplified tile view

B — Simplified Modern Tiles

Rounded tile cards with subtle shadows. Less grid-like, more app-like. Each tile is a small card showing color strip + name. Center area is larger, used for game controls and info. Feels like a modern dashboard.

🎲
YOUR TURN

C — Board + Right Panel Split

Board takes 70% of screen. Right panel (30%) has player cards, dice, turn controls, activity log. Board itself is tight — thin tiles, minimal labels. All interaction happens in the side panel. Better for desktop, works on mobile with drawer.
